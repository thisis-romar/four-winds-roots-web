# Content Model

What content each page needs, where it comes from, and who owns it. This doubles as a **content
collection plan**: a build can't start in earnest until the owner-supplied items are gathered.
With the recommended Astro stack, the structured pieces below become content collections / data
files that a non-technical editor maintains via a git-based CMS.

## Shared / global content (collected once)

| Item | Source / owner | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Canonical business name | Owner | Pick ONE (resolve the "Roots/Four Winds" naming variants) |
| Address, phone, fax | Owner | Must match GBP + directories exactly |
| Hours (weekly + holidays) | Owner | Currently not consistently published anywhere |
| OCP registration / pharmacist names | Owner | Trust signal; verify what can be displayed |
| Logo + brand assets | Designer | Per `brand-direction.md` |
| Photos (storefront, team, optical, interior) | Owner / photographer | Real, local — no stock |
| Google review URL | Owner (GBP) | For review-request CTA |
| Privacy policy, accessibility statement | Owner + advisor | Required (see compliance doc) |

## Per-page content model

### Home
- positioning headline + subhead (from `market-positioning.md`)
- 3 pillars (holistic / personal / convenient)
- service grid (6 services, each: name, one-liner, link)
- holistic + optical highlight blurb
- reputation strip (rating + 2–3 reviews)
- NAP + hours + map

### Each Service page (Refills, Transfers, MedsCheck, Blister, Delivery, Vaccinations)
- service name + plain-language intro (1–2 sentences)
- "who it's for" / eligibility
- "how it works" steps
- primary CTA (form or call/book)
- any cost note (e.g., "blister packaging at no extra cost", "free delivery")
- relevant FAQ (optional, 2–4 Q&A) — good for SEO

### Holistic Wellness  *(differentiator — owner input essential)*
- what holistic care means at Four Winds (herb–drug interaction review, wellness)
- services actually offered (confirm with owner — do NOT invent)
- who it's for + how to start
- careful, non-overclaiming language

### Optical & Wellness Centre  *(differentiator — owner input essential)*
- optical services offered (confirm scope with owner)
- wellness services offered
- how it complements the pharmacy
- CTA

### Seniors & Family Care / Student Pharmacy near York U
- segment-specific framing reusing service content (adherence/delivery for seniors; speed/transfers/
  multilingual for students)
- relevant CTA

### Contact / Location / Hours
- full NAP, fax, hours (+ holidays)
- map embed, parking/transit note
- after-hours / emergency instructions
- contact form

## Patient-education content (for SEO + trust, phase 2)

Plain-language local-relevance topics, owned by a pharmacist/reviewer:
diabetes management, blood pressure, vaccinations (flu/COVID/travel), medication adherence,
seniors' medication safety, minor ailments, student health basics. Each: 300–600 words, original,
reviewed, internally linked to the relevant service page.

## Content governance

- **Accuracy over volume.** Every service/claim must be confirmed by the owner; mark anything
  unconfirmed and do not publish it (the audit's "do not invent services" rule applies to our own
  site too).
- **No unsupported health claims** (compliance + trust).
- **Plain language**, generous formatting, short paragraphs (seniors + mobile).
- A named pharmacist reviews clinical/educational content before publication.

## Open items needing owner confirmation (gaps)

- Exact holistic services and optical/wellness scope.
- Whether compounding is offered (competitors lead with it).
- Whether online booking (MedsCheck/vaccines) will use a scheduler or a form.
- Delivery coverage area and any conditions.
- Canonical business name + final hours.
